Description

The Hobbit is a fantasy novel written by J. R. R. Tolkien and first published in 1937. The story follows Bilbo Baggins, a quiet and comfort-loving hobbit who is unexpectedly drawn into an adventure beyond the borders of his peaceful home, the Shire. Persuaded by the wizard Gandalf and a company of thirteen dwarves, Bilbo joins a quest to reclaim a lost kingdom and its treasure from the dragon Smaug. What begins as an unwanted journey gradually becomes a transformative experience for the protagonist.

Throughout the novel, Tolkien explores themes such as personal growth, courage, and the tension between comfort and adventure. Bilbo evolves from a reluctant participant into a resourceful and morally grounded character, often relying on wit rather than strength. The book is also notable for its rich world-building, introducing readers to Middle-earth, a setting that would later be expanded in The Lord of the Rings. Written in a clear and accessible style, The Hobbit remains a foundational work of modern fantasy literature and continues to be widely read across generations.
